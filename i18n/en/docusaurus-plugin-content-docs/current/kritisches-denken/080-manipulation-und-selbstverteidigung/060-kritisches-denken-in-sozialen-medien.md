# Critical Thinking on Social Media

<!--t src=fc0a4b3e-->
Social media poses particular challenges for critical thinking. Here are some specific strategies:

<!--t src=a3f1719d-->
## Challenges on Social Media

<!--t src=46cf2c99-->
1. **Information overload**: The sheer volume of information makes it hard to distinguish quality from quantity.

<!--t src=711ad707-->
2. **Speed**: The speed at which information is shared makes thorough verification difficult.

<!--t src=af6d9d0c-->
3. **Emotional contagion**: Emotional content spreads faster and can override rational thinking.

<!--t src=deae43a8-->
4. **Algorithms**: Personalised feeds can reinforce echo chambers and filter bubbles.

<!--t src=6e1028ae-->
5. **Anonymity**: The ability to post anonymously can lead to a reduced sense of responsibility.

<!--t src=06cf3273-->
## Strategies for Critical Thinking on Social Media

<!--t src=cd70d4b5-->
1. **Slow scrolling**: Take the time to process content instead of quickly scrolling on.

<!--t src=9a081d6c-->
   *Example*: Read articles in full, not just the headlines, before forming an opinion.

<!--t src=7e8fbb9b-->
2. **Active source checking**: Check the source of a post before considering it credible.

<!--t src=f884aa5d-->
   *Example*: Click on the profile that shared a controversial post in order to assess its credibility.

<!--t src=acd6ad52-->
3. **Emotional pause**: If a post provokes strong emotional reactions, take time to reflect before reacting.

<!--t src=681e75cd-->
   *Example*: If you are angry about a post, wait 10 minutes before commenting or sharing.

<!--t src=dec26ed5-->
4. **Conscious curation**: Actively shape your feed so as to see different perspectives.

<!--t src=b43cab9e-->
   *Example*: Deliberately follow sources with different political orientations.

<!--t src=4044d88e-->
5. **Use fact-checking tools**: Use trustworthy fact-checking websites to verify viral claims.

<!--t src=e203b7c1-->
   *Example*: If you see a surprising claim, search for verifications on sites such as Correctiv.org or Faktenfinder.

<!--t src=75b1a3d6-->
6. **Search for context**: Look for the broader context of quotes or excerpts.

<!--t src=9804533c-->
   *Example*: If you see a short video showing a controversial statement, look for the full interview or the full speech.

<!--t src=8883bec5-->
7. **Reading images critically**: Learn to view visual content critically.

<!--t src=3d0404ea-->
   *Example*: Watch out for signs of image manipulation such as unnatural shadows, distorted proportions or inconsistent lighting.
