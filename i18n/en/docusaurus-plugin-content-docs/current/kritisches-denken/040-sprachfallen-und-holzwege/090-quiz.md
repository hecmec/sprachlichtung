<!-- ## Quiz: Language Traps -->

<!--t src=595cc92b-->
1. **What type of ambiguity is present in the sentence "Visiting professors can be interesting"?**
   a) Lexical ambiguity
   b) Syntactic ambiguity
   c) Referential ambiguity
   d) No ambiguity

<!--t src=e34c5774-->
2. **Which of the following examples best illustrates the concept of vagueness?**
   a) "Bank" can refer to a financial institution or the side of a river.
   b) "He saw the man with the telescope."
   c) "She is a rich person." (Unclear at what level of wealth someone counts as "rich")
   d) "The mind is located in the brain."

<!--t src=46593245-->
3. **Which type of linguistic pitfall is used in the sentence "These freedom fighters were brutally oppressed by the repressive regime"?**
   a) Ambiguity
   b) Vagueness
   c) Loaded language
   d) Category mistake

<!--t src=134d0a7b-->
4. **Which of the following examples contains a category mistake?**
   a) "The table is brown."
   b) "Democracy is angry about the election results."
   c) "This book is interesting."
   d) "Some people are tall."

<!--t src=3d277179-->
5. **Which type of definition is present when a scientist specifies for a study: "We define 'chronic pain' as pain that lasts longer than three months"?**
   a) Reportive definition
   b) Stipulative definition
   c) Precising definition
   d) Persuasive definition

<!--t src=a93d0b93-->
**Answers:**
1. b) Syntactic ambiguity (Unclear whether the professors are visitors or whether it is about the act of visiting professors)
2. c) "She is a rich person." (Unclear at what level of wealth someone counts as "rich")
3. c) Loaded language
4. b) "Democracy is angry about the election results."
5. b) Stipulative definition
