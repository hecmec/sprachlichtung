:::tip In brief
The past appears distorted &mdash; often better or worse, mostly better.

_Things were more glittery in the old days._
:::

<!--t src=23594b0b-->
## Definition

<!--t src=e80609cc-->
**Rosy retrospection** describes the tendency to remember past events in a systematically distorted way &mdash; often more positively (nostalgia, rosy retrospection), but sometimes also more negatively (declinism: "everything used to be better, today everything is worse") than they actually were.

<!--t src=f1e828d1-->
DE: _Vergangenheitsverklärung (Rosy retrospection, Declinism)_

<!--t src=5d2df519-->
## Related Biases

<!--t src=4777cdea-->
Rosy retrospection is closely connected to several other biases and is influenced by them:

<!--t src=29476e92-->
- **Hindsight bias:** In retrospect much seems to have "always been clear" &mdash; this smooths the memory.
- **Peak-end rule:** We mainly remember the highlights and the end, not the average.
- **Negativity bias:** In times of crisis we over-emphasize negative retrospection ("decline").
- **Consistency bias:** We unconsciously adjust memories to current beliefs.
- **Availability heuristic:** Easily retrievable, striking episodes dominate the overall picture.

<!--t src=9dbba01b-->
## Examples

<!--t src=df8f2848-->
### The Golden Childhood

<!--t src=3a4dc8e8-->
Memories of school days or summer holidays blank out boredom, conflict, and failures. What remains is an idealized "back then it was carefree."
Children usually don't have to bear responsibility either, and often live in a colorful world of experiences.

<!--t src=b5a3c393-->
### Music Used to Be Better (but that's actually true)

<!--t src=4cd93907-->
One remembers the highlights of a decade, not the mediocre and trashy share. The present is unfairly compared with the curated past.

<!--t src=8fcf1755-->
### Societal Decline

<!--t src=bfb213ba-->
Current problems are more present and therefore overweighted.
Statistical data, however, often show long-term improvements (health, education, safety) that are underestimated in hindsight.
On the topic of societal decline and why it isn't true, there is a whole body of literature.

<!--t src=90cb0b3f-->
## Effects

<!--t src=f1a4a3dc-->
- Distorted evaluations of developments (exaggerated nostalgia or cultural pessimism)
- Misallocation of attention and resources ("a former state as the goal")
- Resistance to data that show positive trends

<!--t src=87dd61f7-->
## Counter-Strategies

<!--t src=a0f9263d-->
- Systematically compare data (long-term statistics instead of individual anecdotes)
- Contextualize memories: which parts am I blanking out? Which sources shape my picture?
- Use contemporaneous records/journals instead of relying on later memory
- Consider both "highlights" and "average" (avoid the peak-end trap)
