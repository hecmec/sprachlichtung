<!--t src=ae64f240-->
:::tip In brief
The more people watch, the less anyone helps.

_Everyone thought the others were already doing something._
:::

<!--t src=23594b0b-->
## Definition

<!--t src=b9f64fa9-->
The **bystander effect** describes the tendency for the probability of helping to decrease in the presence of many others. The main reasons are **diffusion of responsibility** ("the others will take care of it"), **pluralistic ignorance** (one interprets the passivity of others as a sign that no intervention is needed) and social uncertainty.

<!--t src=b1d141bf-->
EN: _Bystander Effect_

<!--t src=5d2df519-->
## Related biases

<!--t src=f009714f-->
The bystander effect is closely connected with several social biases and is reinforced by them:

<!--t src=58043733-->
- **Diffusion of responsibility:** Responsibility is spread across many shoulders and is not taken on by any individual.
- **Pluralistic ignorance:** From the passivity of others, it is falsely concluded that not acting is appropriate.
- **Conformity pressure/social proof:** One orients oneself to the group, especially in ambiguous situations.
- **Stress/uncertainty bias:** Fear of wrong decisions and of being judged by others inhibits the willingness to act.

<!--t src=9dbba01b-->
## Examples

<!--t src=07299526-->
### An emergency in public

<!--t src=75bc9bc4-->
At an accident on a busy street, passers-by hesitate because each person assumes that "surely someone else is already calling the emergency services".

<!--t src=4baa532d-->
### Online forums and livestreams

<!--t src=3547c625-->
Many see a clear rule violation or hate speech, yet hardly anyone reports it &mdash; everyone assumes that others are reporting it or that moderation will intervene.

<!--t src=c5945995-->
### Workplace/school

<!--t src=17cb2eed-->
Several people observe bullying or a serious process error. No one intervenes, because it is unclear who is responsible and how the group will react.

<!--t src=90cb0b3f-->
## Effects

<!--t src=1357586a-->
- Delayed help, up to dangerous failures to act
- A shift in norms: passivity seems "normal", lowering future willingness to intervene
- Psychological costs: guilt, stress, loss of trust within communities

<!--t src=87dd61f7-->
## Countermeasures

<!--t src=c130787f-->
- Assign responsibility directly: "You in the red jacket, call 112!"
- Train action scripts (first aid, reporting channels, moderation rules)
- Create signals of seriousness: "emergency", "stop", "I'll take over" &mdash; reduce ambiguity
- Make responsibility visible (incident owner, clear roles)
