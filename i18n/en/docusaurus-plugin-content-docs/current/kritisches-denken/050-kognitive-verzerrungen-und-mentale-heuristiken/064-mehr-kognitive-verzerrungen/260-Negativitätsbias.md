:::tip In brief
The negative weighs more heavily than the positive.

_One mistake drowns out ten successes._
:::

<!--t src=23594b0b-->
## Definition

<!--t src=c20fdf02-->
The **negativity bias** is the tendency to weight negative information, events and emotions more heavily, process them more intensely and remember them longer than equally strong positive ones.

<!--t src=2b250850-->
EN: _Negativity Bias_

<!--t src=5d2df519-->
## Related biases

<!--t src=fd1edce8-->
The negativity bias is closely connected with several other biases and is reinforced by them:

<!--t src=dd5c7d08-->
- **Loss aversion:** Losses hurt psychologically more than equally large gains please.
- **Availability heuristic:** Dramatic, negative examples are especially memorable and dominate judgments.
- **Attention bias:** Attention is reflexively directed at dangers and problems.
- **Confirmation bias:** Whoever believes in decline primarily selects negative evidence.
- **Media bias:** News focuses on exceptions/crises &mdash; amplifying the felt negativity.

<!--t src=9dbba01b-->
## Examples

<!--t src=b1062209-->
### News consumption

<!--t src=c905edac-->
Daily headlines about crises, wars and scandals lead to an overly negative worldview, even though long-term data often show improvements.

<!--t src=10c88df5-->
### Performance feedback

<!--t src=0d64bef0-->
In performance reviews, a single critical remark sticks more strongly than several positive points &mdash; motivation suffers.

<!--t src=a285c1a8-->
### Product reviews

<!--t src=235bd3b2-->
A single 1‑star comment influences the purchase decision more than many 5‑star reviews.

<!--t src=90cb0b3f-->
## Effects

<!--t src=bfcd84ce-->
- Pessimistic risk assessment, decision paralysis
- Overreactions to exceptions, neglect of positive trends
- Stress, anxiety, declining satisfaction and relationship quality

<!--t src=87dd61f7-->
## Countermeasures

<!--t src=bc36bc55-->
- Systematically track positive evidence too (e.g. a "success/gratitude journal")
- Look at baselines and time series instead of highlights (base rates)
- A balanced information diet: a variety of sources, deliberate breaks from the news
- Structure feedback: first strengths, then improvements, then next steps
