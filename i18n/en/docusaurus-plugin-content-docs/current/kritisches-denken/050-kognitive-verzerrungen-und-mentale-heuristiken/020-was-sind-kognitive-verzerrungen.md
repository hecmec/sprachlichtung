<!-- ## What Are Cognitive Biases? -->

<!--t src=4b316fe8-->
Cognitive biases (biases, or perceptual errors) are systematic thinking errors of our mind. You and I, we commit these errors constantly, often without noticing.

<!--t src=35bd0886-->
These thinking errors usually arise because we (our minds) try to simplify problems or questions. Our mind mostly does this for reasons of efficiency, and as a rule through heuristics, the so-called cognitive shortcuts.

<!--t src=10fe353b-->
**The consequence**: we tend toward misjudgments, irrational beliefs, or distorted perceptions.
This, however, is exactly how very typical prejudices and pigeonhole thinking arise.

<!--t src=2c29ebeb-->
## Characteristics of Cognitive Biases

<!--t src=88af185d-->
1. **Systematic**: Cognitive biases are not random errors but occur in predictable patterns.

<!--t src=8954a7d0-->
2. **Unconscious**: They mostly operate below the threshold of consciousness and are therefore difficult to recognize.

<!--t src=f5ea950d-->
3. **Universal**: They affect all people, regardless of intelligence or education.

<!--t src=b4b53852-->
4. **Persistent**: Even when we are aware of them, they can continue to influence our thinking.\
But once we know them, we can try to counteract them.

<!--t src=7a709322-->
5. **Adaptive**: Many cognitive biases evolved because they were or are useful in certain environments or situations.

<!--t src=fee86405-->
## Origins of Cognitive Biases

<!--t src=8b1cb4e1-->
Cognitive biases arise for various reasons:

<!--t src=d3461aba-->
1. **Information Processing Limitations**: Our brain has limited capacity for processing information and must therefore take shortcuts.

<!--t src=bfbe9c6e-->
2. **Motivational Factors**: We tend to interpret information in ways that confirm our existing beliefs or desires.

<!--t src=ba9ca00c-->
3. **Social Influences**: Our thinking is influenced by social norms, group dynamics, and the desire for belonging.

<!--t src=460f0e06-->
4. **Evolutionary Adaptations**: Some biases may have provided evolutionary advantages, even if they lead to suboptimal decisions in the modern world.

<!--t src=57d5640a-->
5. **Emotional Influences**: Emotions can override rational thinking and lead to distorted judgments.
