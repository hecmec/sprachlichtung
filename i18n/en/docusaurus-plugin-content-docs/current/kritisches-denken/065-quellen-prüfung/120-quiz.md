## Quiz questions

<!--t src=a5be34bb-->
### 1. **What does the „I“ in the SIFT method stand for?**

<!--t src=773d3928-->
a) Identify the topic
b) Investigate the source
c) Ignore the headline
d) Interpret the data

<!--t src=b9209b64-->
### 2. **What is „lateral reading“?**

<!--t src=95d15326-->
a) Reading a text through slowly several times
b) Judging a page by its logo and design
c) Leaving the page and checking via other sources who is behind it
d) Just skimming the headlines

<!--t src=771ec579-->
### 3. **A person innocently shares a false report that they believe to be true. What is this?**

<!--t src=38b50b7d-->
a) Disinformation
b) Malinformation
c) Misinformation
d) Propaganda

<!--t src=fa270009-->
### 4. **What do social-media recommendation algorithms primarily optimise for?**

<!--t src=8ca6a36e-->
a) The truthfulness of the content
b) Engagement (time spent, clicks, reactions)
c) Balance of opinions
d) The well-being of users

<!--t src=b64f1864-->
### 5. **What does more recent research (e.g. the Reuters Institute) say about the filter-bubble thesis?**

<!--t src=bd4ad434-->
a) It is clearly confirmed
b) It is empirically surprisingly weakly supported; algorithms tend to lead to more diversity
c) Echo chambers affect the great majority of people
d) Filter bubble and echo chamber are the same thing

<!--t src=789fcdc6-->
### 6. **Which question best bundles the check for interests and bias?**

<!--t src=4c2f48d7-->
a) „How old is the source?“
b) „How many likes does the post have?“
c) „Cui bono: who benefits from this information?“
d) „Does the website look professional?“

<!--t src=83ddf95a-->
### 7. **According to this chapter, how should you use Wikipedia?**

<!--t src=24c5f32b-->
a) Not at all, it is unreliable
b) As the sole source, cited directly
c) As a starting point, then following the references to the primary sources
d) Only reading the talk page

<!--t src=5b314868-->
### 8. **What does a „reviewed version“ (gesichtete Version) on the German Wikipedia mean?**

<!--t src=4518622e-->
a) The content has been checked by experts for correctness
b) An experienced user has checked the version for obvious vandalism
c) The article is locked
d) The article is finished and will no longer be changed

<!--t src=1102211a-->
### 9. **What is the fastest method for exposing an „out-of-context“ photo?**

<!--t src=7e608c3a-->
a) Reading the caption several times
b) Checking the number of likes
c) A reverse image search
d) Blindly trusting the EXIF data

<!--t src=e44ccf67-->
### 10. **What is a particularly good way to recognise a reputable fact-checking provider?**

<!--t src=5b9274b9-->
a) By many followers on social media
b) By a commitment to the IFCN code: transparency of sources, funding and methodology, and open corrections
c) By always criticising the same political side
d) By a striking logo

<!--t src=c7ef8a9b-->
## **Answers**

<!--t src=6a46b296-->
1. b) Investigate the source
2. c) Leaving the page and checking via other sources who is behind it
3. c) Misinformation
4. b) Engagement (time spent, clicks, reactions)
5. b) It is empirically surprisingly weakly supported; algorithms tend to lead to more diversity
6. c) „Cui bono: who benefits from this information?“
7. c) As a starting point, then following the references to the primary sources
8. b) An experienced user has checked the version for obvious vandalism
9. c) A reverse image search
10. b) A commitment to the IFCN code
