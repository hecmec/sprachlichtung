Assess your own critical thinking skills by answering the following questions on a scale from 1 (rarely) to 5 (very often):

<!--t src=ba3270fa-->
1. Do you question information before you accept it?
   - [ ] 1 - rarely
   - [ ] 2
   - [ ] 3
   - [ ] 4
   - [ ] 5 - very often

<!--t src=3549d7e9-->
2. Do you actively look for evidence that might contradict your own convictions?
   - [ ] 1 - rarely
   - [ ] 2
   - [ ] 3
   - [ ] 4
   - [ ] 5 - very often

<!--t src=0a4c9aa3-->
3. Do you consider different perspectives before you arrive at a conclusion?
   - [ ] 1 - rarely
   - [ ] 2
   - [ ] 3
   - [ ] 4
   - [ ] 5 - very often

<!--t src=70048c19-->
4. Do you notice when your emotions influence your judgment?
   - [ ] 1 - rarely
   - [ ] 2
   - [ ] 3
   - [ ] 4
   - [ ] 5 - very often

<!--t src=f5dfda42-->
5. Are you willing to change your opinion when new evidence emerges?
   - [ ] 1 - rarely
   - [ ] 2
   - [ ] 3
   - [ ] 4
   - [ ] 5 - very often

<!--t src=18c5b695-->
6. Do you identify assumptions in arguments (your own and others')?
   - [ ] 1 - rarely
   - [ ] 2
   - [ ] 3
   - [ ] 4
   - [ ] 5 - very often

<!--t src=bc64bf8f-->
7. Do you distinguish between facts and opinions?
   - [ ] 1 - rarely
   - [ ] 2
   - [ ] 3
   - [ ] 4
   - [ ] 5 - very often

<!--t src=f5066206-->
8. Do you recognize fallacies in arguments?
   - [ ] 1 - rarely
   - [ ] 2
   - [ ] 3
   - [ ] 4
   - [ ] 5 - very often

<!--t src=1afa8432-->
9. Do you assess the credibility of information sources?
   - [ ] 1 - rarely
   - [ ] 2
   - [ ] 3
   - [ ] 4
   - [ ] 5 - very often

<!--t src=ef433ca6-->
10. Do you regularly reflect on your own thinking processes?
    - [ ] 1 - rarely
    - [ ] 2
    - [ ] 3
    - [ ] 4
    - [ ] 5 - very often

<!--t src=f85f07d5-->
## Scoring:

<!--t src=da02d13a-->
Add up your points based on your answers (1-5 for each question) to get a total score of at most 50.
(Of course you can cheat and rate yourself higher, but that considerably reduces the value of this exercise.)

<!--t src=8979d4cf-->
- 40-50 points: Strong critical thinking skills
- 30-39 points: Good critical thinking skills with room for improvement
- 20-29 points: Basic critical thinking skills that should be developed further
- Below 20 points: Significant potential for developing critical thinking skills

<!--t src=d99422c8-->
Note that this self-assessment is subjective and may be influenced by cognitive biases.
It serves as a starting point for your personal development in critical thinking.
