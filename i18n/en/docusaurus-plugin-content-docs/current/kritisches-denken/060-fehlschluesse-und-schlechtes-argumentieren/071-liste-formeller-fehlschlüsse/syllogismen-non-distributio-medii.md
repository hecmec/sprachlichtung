<!--t src=fc70ff0c-->
:::tip In brief
The middle term does not connect the two premises, because it is fully captured in neither of them.

_A shared property is not identity._
:::

<!--t src=23594b0b-->
## Definition

<!--t src=395af589-->
In the fallacy of the **undistributed middle** (Latin *non distributio medii*), the middle term $M$ – the term that occurs in both premises and is meant to link them – is *distributed* in **neither** of the two premises.

<!--t src=2a375e1d-->
A term is „distributed“ when the statement refers to **all** of its members. In the sentence „All $S$ are $P$“ only the subject $S$ is distributed, not the predicate $P$. If $M$ remains undistributed in both premises, it establishes no connection between the end terms – from a merely *shared* property no identity follows.

<!--t src=4c3eab81-->
The fallacy has the following form ($S$, $P$ = end terms, $M$ = middle term):

<!--t src=786bf2a5-->
1. All $P$ are $M$.
2. All $S$ are $M$.
3. Therefore all $S$ are also $P$.

<!--t src=d5f89f48-->
In both premises $M$ stands as an (undistributed) predicate. The inference would be valid only if $M$ were distributed at least once.

<!--t src=59c18123-->
Also known as: _undistributed middle_

<!--t src=2851dcb3-->
## Related fallacies

<!--t src=4d08b756-->
<!-- list similar fallacies -->

<!--t src=da3e3190-->
- **Quaternio Terminorum** – a related structural error of the syllogism, there by one term too many.
- **Affirming the consequent** – the propositional counterpart: there too one infers from a shared consequence to identity of cause.
- **Hasty generalisation** – substantively related, when from a shared feature identity is inferred.

## Examples

<!--t src=a67a7a61-->
### Example 1

<!--t src=5422e642-->
<!-- description of example one -->

<!--t src=6b6eb649-->
> 1. All lions are animals.
> 2. All cats are animals.
> 3. Therefore all lions are cats.

<!--t src=b077b518-->
The middle term „animals“ is undistributed in both premises (each time it means only *some* animals). That lions and cats are both animals does not make them the same.

<!--t src=f350657d-->
### Example 2

<!--t src=c7d6981c-->
<!-- description of example two -->

<!--t src=1e479c83-->
> „Terrorists use encryption. This app uses encryption. So this app is a tool for terrorists.“

<!--t src=1a89e032-->
„Using encryption“ is the undistributed middle. A shared feature does not establish membership in the same group.
