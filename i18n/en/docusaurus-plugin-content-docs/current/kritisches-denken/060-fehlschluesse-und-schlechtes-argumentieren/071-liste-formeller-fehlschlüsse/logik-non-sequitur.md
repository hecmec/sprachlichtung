:::tip In brief
The conclusion does not follow from the premises, even if these are true.

_It does not follow._
:::

## Definition

<!--t src=0c1429e5-->

**Non sequitur** is Latin for „it does not follow“.
It designates, quite generally, an argument whose conclusion does not result from the premises. Between the assumptions and the conclusion there is no valid logical connection.

<!--t src=4f2469a1-->

In the broad sense, **non sequitur** is the umbrella term for all formal fallacies: every formal fallacy is a special case in which the inference rule is invalid. In the narrow sense, the name is given to an inference whose gap cannot be assigned to any named pattern.

<!--t src=3c24b250-->

The fallacy has the following form:

<!--t src=9f23a9a0-->

1. Premise P (and possibly others).
2. Therefore conclusion K.

<!--t src=409cb8fa-->

… where K is not logically derivable from P. The truth of the premises is irrelevant here: even from true premises a non sequitur can „derive“ a (coincidentally) true or a false conclusion &mdash; in no case is the inference valid.

<!--t src=83ad83e3-->

Also known as: _non sequitur_

<!--t src=2851dcb3-->

## Related fallacies

<!--t src=4d08b756-->
<!-- list similar fallacies -->

<!--t src=743ddeae-->

- **Affirming the consequent** &mdash; a special case of the non sequitur with the conditional.
- **Denying the antecedent** &mdash; likewise a named special case.
- **Argumentum ad Logicam** &mdash; a special form: „the argument is flawed“ does not entail „the claim is false“.
- **Ignoratio elenchi (missing the point)** &mdash; the conclusion is indeed supported, but not the one that is actually at issue.

<!--t src=9dbba01b-->

## Examples

<!--t src=a67a7a61-->

### Example 1

<!--t src=5422e642-->
<!-- description of example one -->

<!--t src=42c449fa-->

> 1. Socrates is a human being.
> 2. The sky is blue.
> 3. Therefore Socrates is mortal.

<!--t src=b5efd4bc-->

The conclusion is indeed true, but it in no way follows from the two premises. A true conclusion does not make an inference valid.

<!--t src=f350657d-->

### Example 2

<!--t src=c7d6981c-->
<!-- description of example two -->

<!--t src=ed9561cc-->

> „She has an expensive car, so she surely understands a great deal about politics.“

<!--t src=95fe5f03-->

Between owning an expensive car and political expertise there is no logical connection. The inference jumps from the premise to an entirely unrelated conclusion.
