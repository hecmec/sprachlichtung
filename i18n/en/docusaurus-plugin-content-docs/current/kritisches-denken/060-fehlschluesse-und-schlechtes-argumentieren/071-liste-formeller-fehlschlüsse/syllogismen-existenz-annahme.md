:::tip In brief
From purely general premises the existence of something is inferred.

_„All“ does not yet say that any exist._
:::

<!--t src=23594b0b-->

## Definition

<!--t src=9d44defd-->

The **existential fallacy** draws, from two **universal** premises („all …“, „no …“), a **particular** conclusion („some …“, „there is …“) that asserts the **existence** of objects.

<!--t src=80aabfff-->

In modern (Boolean) logic, universal statements carry **no existential assumption**: „All $S$ are $P$“ only means „there is no $S$ that is not $P$“ &mdash; and is true even if there are no $S$ at all. From such premises one may therefore not conclude that there is any $S$ at all.

<!--t src=3c24b250-->

The fallacy has the following form:

<!--t src=2fd5fbd8-->

1. All $M$ are $P$. (universal)
2. All $S$ are $M$. (universal)
3. Therefore there is an $S$ that is $P$. (particular, asserting existence)

<!--t src=2824b198-->

Note: on the classical Aristotelian reading such an inference was held to be valid, because there it was tacitly assumed that the terms are not empty. Only the Boolean interpretation makes the fallacy visible.

<!--t src=d99c42aa-->

Also known as: _existential fallacy_

<!--t src=2851dcb3-->

## Examples

<!--t src=a67a7a61-->

### Example 1

<!--t src=5422e642-->
<!-- description of example one -->

<!--t src=b94d03bc-->

> 1. All unicorns have a horn.
> 2. All horn-bearers are animals.
> 3. Therefore there is an animal that is a unicorn.

<!--t src=c52e63a3-->

The premises can be true without a single unicorn existing. The conclusion, however, asserts an existence that is not given.

<!--t src=f350657d-->

### Example 2

<!--t src=c7d6981c-->
<!-- description of example two -->

<!--t src=33f8b465-->

> 1. All inhabitants of a Moon colony pay Moon tax.
> 2. All who pay Moon tax are registered.
> 3. Therefore there are registered Moon-tax payers.

<!--t src=0e514a9d-->

As long as no one lives on the Moon, the premises are vacuously true &mdash; and the existence claim of the conclusion is unfounded.
