<!--t src=66e47425-->

:::tip In brief
From „If A, then B“ and „B is true“ one wrongly concludes „A is true“.

_The effect does not prove the cause._
:::

<!--t src=23594b0b-->

## Definition

<!--t src=dd5eacb8-->

**Affirming the consequent** (also called asserting the consequent) infers, from a conditional and the truth of its consequent, the truth of its antecedent.

<!--t src=2c7db0a7-->
The error lies in the fact that "If A, then B" only says that A is _sufficient_ for B &mdash; not that A is the only possible cause of B. B can also be true for other reasons.

<!--t src=3c24b250-->

The fallacy has the following form:

<!--t src=d1e42aba-->

1. If A, then B.
2. B is true.
3. Therefore A is true.

<!--t src=f69186f7-->

Valid, by contrast, is only the _modus ponens_ (1. If A, then B. 2. A. 3. Therefore B.).

<!--t src=e4be8a70-->

Also known as: _affirming the consequent_

<!--t src=2851dcb3-->

## Related fallacies

<!--t src=4d08b756-->
<!-- list similar fallacies -->

<!--t src=8cbde0ec-->
- **Denying the antecedent** &mdash; the mirror image: there one infers from the negated antecedent to the negated consequent.
- **Converse error** &mdash; the underlying confusion: "If A, then B" is treated like "If B, then A".
- **Non sequitur** &mdash; the umbrella term for all these invalid inferences.

<!--t src=9dbba01b-->

## Examples

<!--t src=a67a7a61-->

### Example 1

<!--t src=5422e642-->
<!-- description of example one -->

<!--t src=36fbcdb7-->

> 1. If it is raining, the street is wet.
> 2. The street is wet.
> 3. Therefore it is raining.

<!--t src=88c7c9d9-->

The street can also be wet because of street cleaning or a burst pipe. The rain does not necessarily follow from the wet street.

<!--t src=f350657d-->

### Example 2

<!--t src=c7d6981c-->
<!-- description of example two -->

<!--t src=ee93d02f-->

> 1. If someone has the flu, they have a fever.
> 2. Anna has a fever.
> 3. Therefore Anna has the flu.

<!--t src=10d0ea80-->

A fever can have many other causes. The inference from the effect (fever) to a particular cause (flu) is not valid.
