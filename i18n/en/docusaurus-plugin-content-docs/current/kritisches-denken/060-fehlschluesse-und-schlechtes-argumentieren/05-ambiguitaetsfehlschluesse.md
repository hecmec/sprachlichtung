<!-- ## Fallacies of Ambiguity -->

<!--t src=50c1a17c-->
Fallacies of ambiguity arise from ambiguities or vagueness in language.

<!--t src=49ffb0e1-->
## 1. Equivocation

<!--t src=e7697e03-->
In equivocation, a word or term is used with different meanings as if it had the same meaning.

<!--t src=0c395378-->
**Example:**
"Only man has rights. Women are not men. Therefore women have no rights."

<!--t src=4546ca09-->
**Why is this flawed?** The word "man" is first used in the sense of "human being", then it is implicitly switched to "male human being".

<!--t src=e629b66c-->
## 2. Amphiboly

<!--t src=e90003ea-->
Amphiboly arises from grammatical ambiguity that can lead to different interpretations.

<!--t src=08314224-->
**Example:**
"Elephant hunting is immoral and should be banned." **Why is this ambiguous?** It is unclear whether this means that elephants should not be hunted, or that elephants should not hunt.

<!--t src=882aba45-->
## 3. Accent

<!--t src=d580ffbb-->
This fallacy arises from incorrect stress or emphasis that changes the meaning of a statement.

<!--t src=9fd6fa64-->
**Example:**
"We should not speak badly about our colleagues." (Depending on which word is stressed, the meaning changes.)

<!--t src=85e7ea59-->
## 4. Composition

<!--t src=e9d7aaf5-->
In the fallacy of composition, it is wrongly assumed that a property of the parts must also be a property of the whole.

<!--t src=40351b56-->
**Example:**
"Every part of this computer is light. Therefore the computer is light."

<!--t src=8d9cd57d-->
**Why is this flawed?** The property "light" of the individual parts does not necessarily transfer to the whole.

<!--t src=244e755c-->
**Brain example**
"The neurons are not visible to the naked eye, so the brain is not visible to the naked eye."

<!--t src=ffb750cf-->
## 5. Division

<!--t src=e66788db-->
The fallacy of division is the opposite of the fallacy of composition: it is wrongly assumed that a property of the whole must also be a property of each part.

<!--t src=6acfa8c9-->
**Example:**
"Germany is a rich country. Therefore every German is rich."

<!--t src=e8b24385-->
**Why is this flawed?** The property "rich" of the country as a whole does not necessarily transfer to each individual citizen.

<!--t src=5b42862e-->
**Brain example**
"The brain can think, so the neurons can think."
