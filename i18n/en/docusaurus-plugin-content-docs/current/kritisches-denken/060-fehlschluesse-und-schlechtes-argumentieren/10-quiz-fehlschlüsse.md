## Quiz questions:

<!--t src=76241ba3-->
### 1. **Which fallacy is present when someone argues: "No one has proven that aliens don't exist, so they must exist"?**

<!--t src=a8fb5116-->
- a) Post hoc ergo propter hoc
- b) Argumentum ad ignorantiam
- c) Petitio principii
- d) False dichotomy

<!--t src=a6fc0b03-->
### 2. **A politician says: "My opponent smoked marijuana in his youth, so you shouldn't take his economic plan seriously." Which fallacy is this?**

<!--t src=d14ce77a-->
- a) Ad hominem
- b) Straw man
- c) Red herring
- d) Slippery slope

<!--t src=7d993d47-->
### 3. **"If we introduce a speed limit on motorways, we will soon live in a totalitarian state in which every aspect of our life is regulated." Which fallacy is this?**

<!--t src=cb473be9-->
- a) Post hoc ergo propter hoc
- b) False dichotomy
- c) Slippery slope
- d) Argumentum ad baculum

<!--t src=a7ef024f-->
### 4. **Which of the following is a formal fallacy?**

<!--t src=c6cc613a-->
- a) Straw man
- b) Affirming the consequent
- c) Argumentum ad populum
- d) Complex question

<!--t src=82ca8cf6-->
### 5. **"All the parts of this computer are cheap. So the computer is cheap." Which fallacy is present here?**

<!--t src=09db93b6-->
- a) Division
- b) Composition
- c) Equivocation
- d) Amphiboly

<!--t src=aefcf85c-->
## Answers:

<!--t src=6ecd15b0-->
1. b) Argumentum ad ignorantiam
2. a) Ad hominem
3. c) Slippery slope
4. b) Affirming the consequent
5. b) Composition
