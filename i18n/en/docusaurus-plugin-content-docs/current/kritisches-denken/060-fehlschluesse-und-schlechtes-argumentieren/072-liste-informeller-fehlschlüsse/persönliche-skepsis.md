<!--t src=0eed4a58-->
:::tip In brief
„I can't imagine that, so it isn't true.“

_The limits of one's own imagination are not the limits of reality._
:::

<!--t src=23594b0b-->
## Definition

<!--t src=2ab01c79-->
In the **argument from personal incredulity**, from the fact that someone **cannot imagine or understand** something, it is concluded that it cannot be **true** (or conversely: because no other explanation comes to mind, the obvious one must be correct).

<!--t src=33400cd5-->
The error: what a person finds unimaginable depends on their knowledge and imagination – not on reality. Much that is true is counterintuitive. Unimaginability is no counter-proof.

<!--t src=3c24b250-->
The fallacy has the following form:

<!--t src=94e720a5-->
1. I cannot imagine / explain B.
2. Therefore B is false (or: therefore the alternative that is obvious to me must be true).

<!--t src=e14aadf5-->
Also known as: _argument from incredulity_ / _personal incredulity_

<!--t src=2851dcb3-->
## Related fallacies

<!--t src=4d08b756-->
<!-- list similar fallacies -->

<!--t src=1981f468-->
- **Argumentum ad Ignorantiam** – related: a conclusion is drawn from not-knowing.
- **God of the gaps argument** – a special form: the explanatory gap is filled with the supernatural.
- **Wishful thinking** – related, when the scepticism serves what one does not *want* to believe.

## Examples

<!--t src=a67a7a61-->
### Example 1

<!--t src=5422e642-->
<!-- description of example one -->

<!--t src=2bf9cb5d-->
> „I can't imagine that such complex creatures arise from simple cells – so evolution can't be correct.“

<!--t src=953f635f-->
The difficulty of imagining a process says nothing about whether it took place.

<!--t src=f350657d-->
### Example 2

<!--t src=c7d6981c-->
<!-- description of example two -->

<!--t src=4f12d24d-->
> „Humans could never have moved such huge stone blocks on their own – aliens must have built that.“

<!--t src=8dcfc354-->
From one's own inability to imagine the technique, no extraterrestrial explanation follows.
