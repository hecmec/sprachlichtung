<!--t src=66092cb7-->

:::tip In brief
A person is disparaged in advance, so that everything they say later appears untrustworthy.

_Poison the well before anyone drinks._
:::

<!--t src=23594b0b-->

## Definition

<!--t src=01817d7c-->
**Poisoning the well** is a preemptive form of the **argumentum ad hominem**: before a person even puts forward an argument, they are saddled with negative (often irrelevant) information, so that the audience rejects them from the outset.

<!--t src=1a06de36-->

The error: what is said about the person does not bear on the truth of what they go on to say. The advance discrediting is meant to prevent a substantive examination.

<!--t src=3c24b250-->

The fallacy has the following form:

<!--t src=df4d48ea-->

1. About person A the negative information E is spread (in advance).
2. Person A then puts forward claim B.
3. Therefore B is untrustworthy from the outset.

<!--t src=1882ecc9-->

Also known as: _poisoning the well_

<!--t src=2851dcb3-->

## Related fallacies

<!--t src=4d08b756-->
<!-- list similar fallacies -->

<!--t src=57f7643e-->
- **Argumentum ad hominem** &mdash; the umbrella term; poisoning the well is the _preemptive_ variant.
- **Suggestive insinuation** &mdash; related: a suspicion is floated without evidence.
- **Genetic fallacy** &mdash; evaluation by source rather than by content.

<!--t src=9dbba01b-->

## Examples

<!--t src=a67a7a61-->

### Example 1

<!--t src=5422e642-->
<!-- description of example one -->

<!--t src=4aebd79d-->
> "Before the previous speaker takes the floor, you should know that he is paid by the industry &mdash; judge for yourselves."

<!--t src=905194f3-->

The payment may give cause for caution, but it does not refute his arguments in advance. The audience is turned against him before he has said anything.

<!--t src=f350657d-->

### Example 2

<!--t src=c7d6981c-->
<!-- description of example two -->

<!--t src=5b3ae18f-->

> „Only someone with no heart could think the following proposal is a good idea.“

<!--t src=b5c2537f-->

Whoever agrees with the proposal afterwards automatically counts as heartless. The statement immunises one's own position against contradiction.
