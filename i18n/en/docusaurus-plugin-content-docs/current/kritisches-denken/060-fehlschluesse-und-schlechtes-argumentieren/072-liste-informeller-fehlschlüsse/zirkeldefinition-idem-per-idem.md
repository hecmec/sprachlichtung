:::tip In brief
A term is defined with the help of itself &mdash; the definition explains nothing.

_The same by the same._
:::

<!--t src=23594b0b-->

## Definition

<!--t src=1f846b1d-->

The **circular definition** (Latin _idem per idem_ &mdash; „the same by the same“) explains a term by **using it (or a synonymous word) again** in the explanation. Whoever does not know the term is afterwards just as wise as before.

<!--t src=f64071fe-->

The error: a definition is supposed to reduce the unknown to the known. If it goes round in a circle, it does not achieve that. It is the definition variant of **circular reasoning**.

<!--t src=3c24b250-->

The fallacy has the following form:

<!--t src=15ffb4ae-->

1. The term X is defined.
2. In the definition X (or a synonym) occurs again.
3. The definition thereby does not explain X.

<!--t src=65ae02b5-->

Also known as: _circular definition_ / _idem per idem_

<!--t src=2851dcb3-->

## Related fallacies

<!--t src=4d08b756-->
<!-- list similar fallacies -->

<!--t src=26452434-->

- **Circular reasoning** &mdash; the umbrella term at the level of argumentation.
- **Distinction without a difference** &mdash; related: words are rearranged without explaining anything.

## Examples

<!--t src=a67a7a61-->

### Example 1

<!--t src=5422e642-->
<!-- description of example one -->

<!--t src=4322cdc4-->

> „Sleep is the state in which one sleeps.“

<!--t src=22cb4605-->

Whoever does not know what sleeping is does not learn it this way. The term is explained by itself.

<!--t src=f350657d-->

### Example 2

<!--t src=c7d6981c-->
<!-- description of example two -->

<!--t src=cdf3f89b-->

> „A crime is a criminal act.“

<!--t src=b13c2376-->

„Criminal“ means nothing other than „relating to a crime“. The definition goes round in a circle.
