:::tip In brief
A general rule is rigidly applied to an exceptional case for which it does not hold.

_The rule has exceptions – the argument does not._
:::

<!--t src=23594b0b-->
## Definition

<!--t src=7788e795-->
In the **accident fallacy** (Latin *a dicto simpliciter ad dictum secundum quid* – „from what is said simply to what is said with qualification“), a **general rule** that holds in the normal case is transferred to a **special case** to which it precisely does not apply.

<!--t src=071caccf-->
The error: general rules hold „as a rule“ and admit of justified exceptions. Whoever overlooks the exceptional character of a case draws a false conclusion from a correct rule.

<!--t src=3c24b250-->
The fallacy has the following form:

<!--t src=5e62f7c4-->
1. The rule R holds generally.
2. The case F is an exception to R.
3. R is nevertheless applied to F.

<!--t src=5d13f1e6-->
Also known as: _fallacy of accident_ / _a dicto simpliciter_

<!--t src=2851dcb3-->
## Related fallacies

<!--t src=4d08b756-->
<!-- list similar fallacies -->

<!--t src=447772ce-->
- **Abuse of a principle** – very closely related: rigid application of a rule outside its domain of validity.
- **Hasty generalisation** – the counterpart (*converse accident*): from a special case a general rule is made.
- **No true Scotsman** – a related game with rules and exceptions.

## Examples

<!--t src=a67a7a61-->
### Example 1

<!--t src=5422e642-->
<!-- description of example one -->

<!--t src=4db59675-->
> „Cutting into someone with a knife is bodily harm. A surgeon cuts with a scalpel. So the surgeon commits bodily harm.“

<!--t src=e278a9fe-->
The rule holds for ordinary cases, not for the healing operation carried out with consent. The exceptional character is overlooked.

<!--t src=f350657d-->
### Example 2

<!--t src=c7d6981c-->
<!-- description of example two -->

<!--t src=6fd48ffe-->
> „One should tell the truth. So you must tell the furious pursuer where his victim is hiding.“

<!--t src=52e401f5-->
The rule „tell the truth“ does not cover this emergency. Its rigid application leads to something reprehensible.
