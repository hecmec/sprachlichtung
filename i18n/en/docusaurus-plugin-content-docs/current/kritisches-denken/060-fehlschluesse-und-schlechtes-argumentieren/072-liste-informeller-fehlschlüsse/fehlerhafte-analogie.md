<!--t src=692a5ed1-->
:::tip In brief
From a similarity in one respect, agreement in a quite different respect is inferred.

_Similar is not the same._
:::

<!--t src=23594b0b-->
## Definition

<!--t src=e16222f9-->
In the **false analogy**, from the similarity of two things in **one** feature it is inferred that they also agree in **another, decisive** feature – although precisely on this point **relevant differences** exist.

<!--t src=1900a1da-->
The error: analogies can illuminate, but they prove nothing if the compared cases differ precisely on the point at issue. What matters is whether the similarity is *relevant* to the conclusion drawn.

<!--t src=3c24b250-->
The fallacy has the following form:

<!--t src=a70ca8b1-->
1. A and B resemble each other in feature M1.
2. A also has feature M2.
3. Therefore B too has feature M2.

<!--t src=baf3a3fe-->
… although A and B differ substantially precisely with respect to M2.

<!--t src=88fe6b65-->
Also known as: _false analogy_ / _weak analogy_

<!--t src=2851dcb3-->
## Related fallacies

<!--t src=4d08b756-->
<!-- list similar fallacies -->

<!--t src=c5547e06-->
- **Hasty generalisation** – related: inadmissible transfer to another case.
- **Abuse of a principle** – related, when a rule is transferred to a non-comparable case.

<!--t src=9dbba01b-->
## Examples

<!--t src=a67a7a61-->
### Example 1

<!--t src=5422e642-->
<!-- description of example one -->

<!--t src=9a7ff86a-->
> „A state is like a family: the father decides, the children obey. So the citizens too should simply obey the government.“

<!--t src=4253c38a-->
State and family resemble each other in some respects, but differ precisely on the decisive points (the citizens' autonomy, the rule of law). The analogy does not carry the conclusion.

<!--t src=f350657d-->
### Example 2

<!--t src=c7d6981c-->
<!-- description of example two -->

<!--t src=9ea7b788-->
> „The body sometimes needs an incision when ill – so society needs harsh cuts when it has problems.“

<!--t src=a67b987f-->
Transferring the medical imagery to society presupposes a comparability that is not given.
