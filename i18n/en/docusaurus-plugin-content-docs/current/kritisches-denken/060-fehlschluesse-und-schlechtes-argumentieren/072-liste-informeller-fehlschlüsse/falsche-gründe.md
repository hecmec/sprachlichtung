<!--t src=ee332cb2-->

:::tip In brief
A (perhaps correct) conclusion is supported by reasons that do not support it at all.

_The right thing for the wrong reasons._
:::

<!--t src=23594b0b-->

## Definition

<!--t src=0df6743e-->
In the fallacy of **false reasons** (irrelevant justification), a claim is supported with reasons that are **untrue or irrelevant to the matter**. The conclusion may be correct &mdash; but not _because of_ the reasons given.

<!--t src=1f5dc062-->
The error: a correct conclusion is not well justified merely by naming _some_ reasons. A bad argument remains bad, even if the result is correct.

<!--t src=3c24b250-->

The fallacy has the following form:

<!--t src=bcb8ef14-->

1. Claim B is supported by reason G.
2. G is false or irrelevant to B.
3. B nevertheless counts as justified.

<!--t src=0623066c-->

Also known as: _irrelevant reason_ / _bad reasons_

<!--t src=2851dcb3-->

## Related fallacies

<!--t src=4d08b756-->
<!-- list similar fallacies -->

<!--t src=025aec18-->
- **Non sequitur** &mdash; related: the conclusion does not follow from the premises.
- **Argumentum ad logicam** &mdash; the counterpart: there, from _bad_ reasons, _falsehood_ is inferred.
- **Cherry picking** &mdash; related, when only seemingly supporting reasons are selected.

<!--t src=9dbba01b-->

## Examples

<!--t src=a67a7a61-->

### Example 1

<!--t src=5422e642-->
<!-- description of example one -->

<!--t src=88417844-->

> „You shouldn't smoke, because cigarettes are radioactive.“

<!--t src=bf0c7977-->
That one should not smoke is true &mdash; but the reason given (radioactivity) is not the actual one and does not carry the judgment. It is a false reason for a correct conclusion.

<!--t src=f350657d-->

### Example 2

<!--t src=c7d6981c-->
<!-- description of example two -->

<!--t src=d09b98c2-->

> „This bridge is safe, because it was freshly painted only yesterday.“

<!--t src=9150357c-->

Fresh paint says nothing about load-bearing capacity. Even if the bridge is safe, this reason does not support the statement.
