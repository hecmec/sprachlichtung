:::tip In brief
Instead of refuting the argument, the person who puts it forward is attacked.

_Hit the messenger, not the message._
:::

<!--t src=23594b0b-->

## Definition

<!--t src=302a12a1-->

In the **Argumentum ad Hominem** („argument against the person“), the attack is directed not against the _claim_ but against the _person_ who makes it. The person's characteristics, origin, character or circumstances are meant to invalidate the claim.

<!--t src=01158549-->

This is a fallacy, because the truth of a statement does not depend on **who** utters it. Even an unsympathetic or untrustworthy person can be right.

<!--t src=3c24b250-->

The fallacy has the following form:

<!--t src=e05e49d9-->

1. Person A holds claim B.
2. Person A has the (negative) characteristic E.
3. Therefore B is false.

<!--t src=e21e5209-->

To be distinguished from this is the _legitimate_ criticism of the credibility of a witness &mdash; there it is not about the truth of the statement, but about its reliability as a source.

<!--t src=9981028e-->

Also known as: _ad hominem_

<!--t src=2851dcb3-->

## Related fallacies

<!--t src=4d08b756-->
<!-- list similar fallacies -->

<!--t src=dce4012f-->

- **Tu quoque** &mdash; a special form: the charge of hypocrisy instead of an answer on the merits.
- **Poisoning the well** &mdash; a special form: the person is discredited _in advance_.
- **Genetic fallacy** &mdash; related: judging by origin rather than content.

<!--t src=9dbba01b-->

## Examples

<!--t src=a67a7a61-->

### Example 1

<!--t src=5422e642-->
<!-- description of example one -->

<!--t src=2526f38c-->

> &mdash; „The climate scientist has presented her measurement data.“
> &mdash; „Oh, she's a bigoted activist &mdash; you don't need to take that seriously.“

<!--t src=dcc8e597-->

Instead of examining the data presented, the person is branded an „activist“. An insult refutes no data.

<!--t src=f350657d-->

### Example 2

<!--t src=c7d6981c-->
<!-- description of example two -->

<!--t src=f29bb817-->

> „What does he know about economics? He never even went to university.“

<!--t src=6600aa25-->

Instead of examining the statement, the person's education is attacked. A missing degree does not make a statement false.
