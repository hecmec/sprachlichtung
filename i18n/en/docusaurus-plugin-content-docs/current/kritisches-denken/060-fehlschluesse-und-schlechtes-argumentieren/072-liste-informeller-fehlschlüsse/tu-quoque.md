:::tip In brief
A charge is fended off by holding the same behaviour against the other person.

_„You do it yourself“ refutes nothing._
:::

<!--t src=23594b0b-->

## Definition

<!--t src=8cfdf7cd-->

**Tu quoque** (Latin „you too“) fends off a criticism not with substantive reasons, but with the counter-charge that the criticising person behaves no better themselves. It is a special form of the **Argumentum ad Hominem**.

<!--t src=756e7a00-->

The error: whether a statement or demand is justified does not depend on whether the person who voices it consistently abides by it. Even a hypocrite can be right on the merits.

<!--t src=3c24b250-->

The fallacy has the following form:

<!--t src=730da7d1-->

1. Person A charges person B with the behaviour or claim X.
2. Person A does not themselves act in accordance with X.
3. Therefore the charge (or claim) is void.

<!--t src=b09c794a-->

Also known as: _tu quoque_ / _appeal to hypocrisy_

<!--t src=2851dcb3-->

## Related fallacies

<!--t src=4d08b756-->
<!-- list similar fallacies -->

<!--t src=69da7c8b-->

- **Argumentum ad Hominem** &mdash; the umbrella term: attack on the person instead of the matter.
- **Whataboutism** &mdash; closely related: distraction through counter-charges.
- **Red herring** &mdash; the counter-charge leads away from the topic.

<!--t src=9dbba01b-->

## Examples

<!--t src=a67a7a61-->

### Example 1

<!--t src=5422e642-->
<!-- description of example one -->

<!--t src=b6d275b0-->

> &mdash; „You should smoke less, it's unhealthy.“
> &mdash; „But you drink wine every evening yourself!“

<!--t src=3366da1d-->

The reference to drinking changes nothing about the fact that smoking is unhealthy. The charge is not answered, but redirected.

<!--t src=f350657d-->

### Example 2

<!--t src=c7d6981c-->
<!-- description of example two -->

<!--t src=a72d88d8-->

> &mdash; „This country violates human rights.“
> &mdash; „And you? You have police violence too!“

<!--t src=0db0d622-->

Even if the counter-charge were true, it would not invalidate the original criticism.
