<!--t src=49709f58-->
:::tip In brief
A first step is rejected because it allegedly leads inevitably to ever worse consequences.

_One step, and everything slides._
:::

<!--t src=23594b0b-->
## Definition

<!--t src=959803e7-->
In the **slippery slope** argument, it is claimed that a comparatively harmless first step leads **inevitably** to a chain of ever worse consequences – without this inevitability being justified.

<!--t src=c2473427-->
The error: the individual links of the chain of consequences are neither substantiated nor examined for their probability. „A could lead to B“ is illegitimately turned into „A leads inexorably all the way to Z“. (Where the chain is in fact justified, it is no fallacy.)

<!--t src=3c24b250-->
The fallacy has the following form:

<!--t src=527a31ce-->
1. If we allow A, then B follows, then C … up to the terrible Z.
2. Z is unacceptable.
3. Therefore we must not allow even A.

<!--t src=638e943c-->
Also known as: _slippery slope_

<!--t src=2851dcb3-->
## Related fallacies

<!--t src=4d08b756-->
<!-- list similar fallacies -->

<!--t src=53574f78-->
- **Argumentum ad Baculum** – related: agreement through stoked fear of consequences.
- **Appeal to relative triviality** – the opposite direction: there a step is played down.
- **False dilemma** – related, when only „allow everything“ or „allow nothing“ appears.

<!--t src=9dbba01b-->
## Examples

<!--t src=a67a7a61-->
### Example 1

<!--t src=5422e642-->
<!-- description of example one -->

<!--t src=39abf81e-->
> „If we allow same-sex marriages, soon marriages between several people and finally with animals will be demanded.“

<!--t src=2e356d37-->
The asserted chain of consequences is not justified. Each step would have to be substantiated separately; that does not happen.

<!--t src=f350657d-->
### Example 2

<!--t src=c7d6981c-->
<!-- description of example two -->

<!--t src=de484bb8-->
> „If you allow the child an exception today, tomorrow it will have no rules at all and will later turn criminal.“

<!--t src=ca756cd5-->
From a single exception to criminality is a long, unsubstantiated path. The dramatisation replaces the argument.
