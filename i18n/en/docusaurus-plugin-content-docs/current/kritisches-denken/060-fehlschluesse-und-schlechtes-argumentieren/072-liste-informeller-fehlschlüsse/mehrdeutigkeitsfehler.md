<!--t src=4583c2df-->
:::tip In brief
A sentence allows several readings through its construction; the unsuitable one is exploited.

_Not the word, the sentence structure is ambiguous._
:::

<!--t src=23594b0b-->
## Definition

<!--t src=b28a1c34-->
In the **ambiguity fallacy** – when the ambiguity arises through **sentence structure** one speaks of **amphiboly** – it is not a single word that is ambiguous, but the **grammatical structure** of a sentence that allows several readings. From the ambiguous formulation the *unintended* (or convenient) meaning is read out.

<!--t src=2c008f88-->
The error: the statement supports a conclusion only under *one* reading; the argument, however, uses *another*. While **equivocation** plays out at the word level, amphiboly plays out at the sentence level.

<!--t src=3c24b250-->
The fallacy has the following form:

<!--t src=d7f37366-->
1. The sentence S allows the readings L1 and L2.
2. L1 was meant.
3. The argument, however, uses L2.

<!--t src=22a343cc-->
Also known as: _amphiboly_ / _syntactic ambiguity_

<!--t src=2851dcb3-->
## Related fallacies

<!--t src=4d08b756-->
<!-- list similar fallacies -->

<!--t src=84143c67-->
- **Equivocation** – the counterpart at the word level (one word, two meanings).
- **Insinuation** – related, when the ambiguity is used deliberately to suggest something.

<!--t src=9dbba01b-->
## Examples

<!--t src=a67a7a61-->
### Example 1

<!--t src=5422e642-->
<!-- description of example one -->

<!--t src=99d57056-->
> „He hated his father when he was drinking.“

<!--t src=6b47f9a8-->
Who was drinking – the father or the son? The sentence structure allows both. On whichever reading suits, a whole (fallacious) argument can be built.

<!--t src=f350657d-->
### Example 2

<!--t src=c7d6981c-->
<!-- description of example two -->

<!--t src=34a680fd-->
> „Volunteers needed to help torture survivors.“

<!--t src=68737838-->
The structure allows the reading that one should help to „torture survivors“. The opposite is meant – the ambiguity arises from the sentence structure.
