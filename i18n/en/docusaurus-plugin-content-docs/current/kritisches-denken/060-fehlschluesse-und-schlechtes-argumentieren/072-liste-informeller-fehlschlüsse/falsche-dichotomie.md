A domain is divided into just two mutually exclusive classes, although there are intermediate stages.

_Only black or white, no grey._

<!--t src=23594b0b-->
## Definition

<!--t src=2d3b21b9-->
The **false dichotomy** (black-and-white thinking) divides a range of possibilities into **two mutually exclusive and supposedly exhaustive classes**, although reality knows a **continuum** or several gradations.

<!--t src=0513cfe8-->
It is the conceptual basis of the **false dilemma**: while the dilemma uses the two-way split *in the argument* to force a choice, the dichotomy designates the inadmissible *two-way split itself*.

<!--t src=25fad658-->
The error: the world is rarely two-valued. Whoever knows only „all or nothing“ overlooks the shades of grey in between.

<!--t src=3c24b250-->
The fallacy has the following form:

<!--t src=850fc0cf-->
1. Everything is either A or the opposite of A.
2. Intermediate stages and further categories are blanked out.

<!--t src=17d1d424-->
Also known as: _false dichotomy_ / _black-and-white thinking_

<!--t src=2851dcb3-->
## Related fallacies

<!--t src=4d08b756-->
<!-- list similar fallacies -->

<!--t src=7a164ae3-->
- **False dilemma** – the argumentative application of the two-way split.
- **Sorites paradox** – the counterpart: there the *vagueness* of the boundary is overstretched.
- **Distinction without a difference** – a related game with categories.

## Examples

<!--t src=a67a7a61-->
### Example 1

<!--t src=5422e642-->
<!-- description of example one -->

<!--t src=5922f74e-->
> „Whoever is not for the reform is against progress.“

<!--t src=b2f0ac0b-->
Between „fully in favour“ and „against progress“ lie many attitudes. The division into only two camps is false.

<!--t src=f350657d-->
### Example 2

<!--t src=c7d6981c-->
<!-- description of example two -->

<!--t src=023d8586-->
> „One is either healthy or ill.“

<!--t src=6409a871-->
Health is a continuum with many intermediate states. The strict two-way split misses reality.
