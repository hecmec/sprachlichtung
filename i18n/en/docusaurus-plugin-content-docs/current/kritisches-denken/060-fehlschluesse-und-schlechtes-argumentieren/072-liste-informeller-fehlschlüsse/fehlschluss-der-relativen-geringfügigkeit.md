:::tip In brief
A contribution or harm is declared irrelevant by pointing to its small size.

_„That little bit“ adds up._
:::

<!--t src=23594b0b-->

## Definition

<!--t src=89b99e8f-->
In the **fallacy of relative privation**, a problem, contribution or harm is dismissed because it is **small in itself** &mdash; whether in comparison to "something worse" (relative privation) or because the individual case hardly carries any weight.

<!--t src=ee4c3451-->

The error: the triviality of a single contribution says nothing about the **sum** or the **principle**. Many small contributions produce large effects; and that there are worse things does not make the smaller one harmless.

<!--t src=3c24b250-->

The fallacy has the following form:

<!--t src=5e9134c9-->

1. The contribution/harm X is (in itself) small.
2. Therefore X is insignificant / not a problem.
3. (Often:) There are worse things anyway.

<!--t src=0d1c6606-->
EN: _appeal to relative triviality_ / _minimization_ (special form: _relative privation_ &mdash; "there are worse things")

<!--t src=2851dcb3-->

## Related fallacies

<!--t src=4d08b756-->
<!-- list similar fallacies -->

<!--t src=4ba4b139-->
- **Slippery slope** &mdash; the opposite direction: there a small step is dramatized beyond all measure.
- **Sorites paradox** &mdash; related: the question of when many small things start to count.
- **Red herring** &mdash; related, when "there are worse things" steers away from the topic.

<!--t src=9dbba01b-->

## Examples

<!--t src=a67a7a61-->

### Example 1

<!--t src=5422e642-->
<!-- description of example one -->

<!--t src=63922da0-->

> „One little glass won't hurt.“

<!--t src=d6c5c9a0-->
A single glass may be little in itself &mdash; drunk regularly it adds up. The triviality of the individual case conceals the effect of the habit.

<!--t src=f350657d-->

### Example 2

<!--t src=c7d6981c-->
<!-- description of example two -->

<!--t src=1c04943b-->

> „Why are you getting worked up about this injustice? Elsewhere people are starving.“

<!--t src=8ab8667c-->

That there are bigger problems does not make the smaller one trivial. Pointing to something worse does not refute the objection.
