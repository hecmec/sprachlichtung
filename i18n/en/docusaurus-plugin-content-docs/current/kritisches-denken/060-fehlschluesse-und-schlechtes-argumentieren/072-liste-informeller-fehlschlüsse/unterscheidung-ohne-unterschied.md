:::tip In brief
A verbal distinction is claimed that makes no difference in substance.

_Different words, the same thing._
:::

<!--t src=23594b0b-->

## Definition

<!--t src=a7a7c2da-->

The **distinction without a difference** claims a difference between two things that differ only in **wording**, not in **substance**. The rewording is meant to invalidate a charge or rescue a position, although nothing changes in content.

<!--t src=cbfa83d3-->

The error: two descriptions of the same state of affairs are presented as if they designated different things. The „difference“ is purely verbal.

<!--t src=3c24b250-->

The fallacy has the following form:

<!--t src=4d849cea-->

1. It is claimed that X is something different from Y.
2. X and Y are substantively the same (only named differently).
3. From the apparent difference an advantage is drawn.

<!--t src=390e96d8-->

Also known as: _distinction without a difference_

<!--t src=2851dcb3-->

## Related fallacies

<!--t src=4d08b756-->
<!-- list similar fallacies -->

<!--t src=6b0b799c-->

- **No true Scotsman** &mdash; related: redefinition after the fact to rescue a claim.
- **Equivocation** &mdash; related: playing with word meanings.
- **Special pleading** &mdash; related, when the apparent difference is meant to ground an exception.

<!--t src=9dbba01b-->

## Examples

<!--t src=a67a7a61-->

### Example 1

<!--t src=5422e642-->
<!-- description of example one -->

<!--t src=64a4d014-->

> „I'm not a racist &mdash; I just don't like certain groups of people.“

<!--t src=3f8dc2fb-->

„Not liking certain groups“ is exactly what is meant by racism here. The rewording names no genuine difference.

<!--t src=f350657d-->

### Example 2

<!--t src=c7d6981c-->
<!-- description of example two -->

<!--t src=3a428f5e-->

> „I took a drag on the joint, but didn't inhale.“

<!--t src=177fbe95-->

For the question of whether someone used the drug, this distinction makes no substantively relevant difference. It serves only to gloss over.
