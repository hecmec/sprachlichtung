<!-- # Quiz: What is a good argument? -->

<!--t src=f0bbd90c-->
## Quiz questions:

<!--t src=fbe2ff36-->
### 1. **Which of the following characteristics is NOT a hallmark of a good argument?**

<!--t src=9d9c1c5a-->
- a) Clarity
- b) Emotional impact
- c) Relevance
- d) Adequacy

<!--t src=35615b31-->
### 2. **What is the main difference between rhetoric and argumentation?**

<!--t src=343934b3-->
- a) Rhetoric is always manipulative, argumentation never is.
- b) Rhetoric aims at emotional persuasion, argumentation at rational persuasion.
- c) Rhetoric is used in politics, argumentation in science.
- d) Rhetoric is oral, argumentation is written.

<!--t src=941a389b-->
### 3. **Which type of reasoning is present when one infers the most probable explanation from an observation?**

<!--t src=e5b99abb-->
- a) Deduction
- b) Induction
- c) Abduction
- d) Analogy

<!--t src=ac7c588f-->
### 4. **Which of the following examples illustrates an argument from analogy?**

<!--t src=1e6968cb-->
- a) "All humans are mortal. Socrates is a human. So Socrates is mortal."
- b) "Computers work similarly to the human brain. Computers cannot solve certain tasks. So the human brain probably cannot solve these tasks either."
- c) "Either it is raining, or the street has been sprayed with a water hose. The street is wet. So it has rained."
- d) "If it rains, the street gets wet. The street is wet. So it has rained."

<!--t src=0caa2bdf-->
### 5. **Which method is best suited to uncover hidden value assumptions in an argument?**

<!--t src=d726bb08-->
- a) Gap analysis
- b) Change of perspective
- c) Counterexample test
- d) Conceptual analysis

<!--t src=aefcf85c-->
## Answers:

<!--t src=0cebcae4-->
1. b) Emotional impact
2. b) Rhetoric aims at emotional persuasion, argumentation at rational persuasion.
3. c) Abduction
4. b) "Computers work similarly to the human brain. Computers cannot solve certain tasks. So the human brain probably cannot solve these tasks either."
5. b) Change of perspective
