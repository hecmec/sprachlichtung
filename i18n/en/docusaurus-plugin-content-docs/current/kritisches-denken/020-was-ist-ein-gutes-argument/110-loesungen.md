## Solution to Argument 1:

<!--t src=bde300d4-->
- **Premises**: "Most politicians are corrupt." "Hans is a politician."
- **Conclusion**: "Hans is probably corrupt."
- **Hidden assumptions**: Corruption is evenly distributed among politicians; there are no special factors that distinguish Hans from other politicians.
- **Type of argument**: Inductive (statistical argument)
- **Strength**: Medium &mdash; the conclusion follows with a certain probability, but not with certainty.
- **Possible improvements**: Include more specific information about Hans; define the term "corrupt" more precisely.

## Solution to Argument 2:

<!--t src=d3146dda-->
- **Premises**: "Either we lower taxes, or the economy will collapse." "We cannot afford for the economy to collapse."
- **Conclusion**: "We must lower taxes."
- **Hidden assumptions**: There are only these two options; lowering taxes would actually prevent an economic collapse.
- **Type of argument**: Deductive (disjunctive syllogism)
- **Strength**: Weak &mdash; the first premise represents a false dichotomy.
- **Possible improvements**: Consider further options; cite empirical evidence for the connection between tax policy and economic performance.

## Solution to Argument 3:

<!--t src=c69f68af-->
- **Premises**: "For the last three years it has always rained on the first weekend in July."
- **Conclusion**: "It will rain on the first weekend in July this year too."
- **Hidden assumptions**: Past weather patterns continue into the future; three years are a sufficient sample.
- **Type of argument**: Inductive (generalization from examples)
- **Strength**: Weak &mdash; the sample is too small, and weather patterns are complex and variable.
- **Possible improvements**: Include longer-term weather data; take into account meteorological factors that influence the weather.

## Solution to Argument 4:

<!--t src=aec0416b-->
- **Premises**: "Studies have shown that people who meditate regularly experience less stress." "Maria wants to reduce her stress."
- **Conclusion**: "Maria should meditate regularly."
- **Hidden assumptions**: What works for most people will also work for Maria; meditation is the best or only method of reducing stress for Maria.
- **Type of argument**: Practical argument (means-end reasoning)
- **Strength**: Medium &mdash; the conclusion is plausible, but not compelling.
- **Possible improvements**: Consider other methods of stress reduction; include Maria's specific situation and preferences.

## Solution to Argument 5:

<!--t src=2473e564-->
- **Premises**: "If the sun is shining, the snow will melt." "The snow is melting."
- **Conclusion**: "The sun is shining."
- **Hidden assumptions**: Sunshine is the only cause of melting snow.
- **Type of argument**: Deductive (attempts to be modus ponens, but commits the fallacy of affirming the consequent)
- **Strength**: Weak &mdash; logically invalid, since there can be other causes of melting snow (e.g. warm temperatures without direct sunshine, rain, artificial heat sources).
- **Possible improvements**: Reformulate it as an abductive argument: "The snow is melting. One possible explanation is that the sun is shining. If no other factors are present, the sun is probably shining."
