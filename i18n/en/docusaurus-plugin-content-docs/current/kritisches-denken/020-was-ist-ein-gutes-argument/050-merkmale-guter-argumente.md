<!-- # Characteristics of good arguments -->

<!--t src=0890483b-->
A good argument is distinguished by several quality characteristics, which we will examine more closely in what follows.

<!--t src=4b047ffe-->
## Clarity

<!--t src=9a1448bf-->
A good argument is clearly formulated. This means:

<!--t src=69f5c092-->
- The terms used are precisely defined and used consistently.
- The structure of the argument is recognizable: premises and conclusion can be clearly distinguished.
- Complex ideas are broken down into understandable parts.

<!--t src=b3784d60-->
### Example of a clear argument:

<!--t src=188c8259-->
> Since all mammals have lungs (premise 1) and whales are mammals (premise 2), whales have lungs (conclusion).

<!--t src=1daae14d-->
### Example of an unclear argument:

<!--t src=1090e733-->
> Whales are somehow different from fish, so they must breathe in a different way.

<!--t src=3992f3aa-->
## Relevance

<!--t src=435e55ad-->
The premises of a good argument are relevant to the conclusion. They actually contribute to supporting the conclusion.

<!--t src=0724d835-->
### Example of an argument with relevant premises:

<!--t src=61d7f901-->
> This treatment method has shown a success rate of 85% in clinical studies with thousands of patients. Therefore it is probably effective for your case as well.

<!--t src=594f4122-->
### Example of an argument with irrelevant premises:

<!--t src=3186f118-->
> This treatment method is recommended by a famous doctor who has already treated many celebrities. Therefore it is probably effective for your case as well.

<!--t src=0c5f9ae8-->
## Adequacy

<!--t src=163b2e28-->
The premises of a good argument are adequate, i.e. they are sufficient to support the conclusion, and they are themselves well justified or plausible.

<!--t src=568731ec-->
### Example of an argument with adequate premises:

<!--t src=471af920-->
> The average temperature on Earth has risen by 1°C over the last 100 years (well-documented fact). Human activities have increased the CO2 content in the atmosphere (well-documented fact). CO2 is a greenhouse gas that stores heat in the atmosphere (scientifically established mechanism). Therefore human activities contribute to global warming.

<!--t src=81629ba8-->
### Example of an argument with inadequate premises:

<!--t src=fe0301c6-->
> It was especially cold in my town last winter. Therefore global warming is a myth.

<!--t src=fa5b2f6d-->
## Refutability

<!--t src=aea038a2-->
A good argument is in principle refutable, i.e. there are possible conditions under which it could be shown to be false. Arguments that are formulated in such a way that they cannot be refuted under any circumstances are often problematic.

<!--t src=f58bc103-->
### Example of a refutable argument:

<!--t src=50dd5c18-->
> All swans observed so far were white. Therefore probably all swans are white." (This argument could be refuted by observing a non-white swan.)

<!--t src=b48e5368-->
### Example of a non-refutable argument:

<!--t src=bf4eebad-->
> There are invisible spirits that disappear as soon as one tries to measure or observe them." (This argument is constructed in such a way that it cannot be refuted.)

<!--t src=60241efb-->
## Proportionality

<!--t src=3943bc18-->
A good argument takes into account the strength of the conclusion in relation to the strength of the premises. The stronger or more far-reaching the claim, the stronger the evidence that is needed.

<!--t src=f0e86698-->
### Example of a proportionate argument:

<!--t src=d0ca219a-->
> Several controlled studies have shown that this medication is effective in about 60% of patients with this condition. Therefore it could be an option for your treatment, although there is no guarantee of success.

<!--t src=a84e40b6-->
### Examples of a disproportionate argument:

<!--t src=e4ba3e37-->
> My neighbor took this medication and felt better. Therefore it will definitely work for you too and eliminate all your symptoms.

<!--t src=6a88fb81-->
> I smoke because it isn't really that dangerous. My grandma smoked all her life and died at 96. My neighbor, a vegetarian jogger, kicked the bucket at 45.
