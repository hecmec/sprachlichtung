# Renewable Energy — Curated Resources / Ressources / Ressourcen

A trilingual collection of reliable resources to **understand, learn, and teach** renewable energy.

---

## 🇬🇧 English

### Authoritative data & reports

- [IRENA – International Renewable Energy Agency](https://www.irena.org/) — global statistics, country profiles, technology briefs
- [IEA – International Energy Agency](https://www.iea.org/) — reports, data, free explainers
- [NREL – US National Renewable Energy Laboratory](https://www.nrel.gov/) — deep technical resources and datasets
- [Our World in Data – Energy](https://ourworldindata.org/energy) — free charts, ideal for teaching trends
- [US EIA – Energy Explained](https://www.eia.gov/energyexplained/) — clear, structured explainers

### Free courses

- [edX – Energy courses](https://www.edx.org/learn/energy) — from TU Delft, DTU, and others
- [Coursera – Renewable Energy](https://www.coursera.org/courses?query=renewable%20energy)
- [MIT OpenCourseWare](https://ocw.mit.edu/)
- [TU Delft OpenCourseWare](https://ocw.tudelft.nl/)

### Teaching-oriented

- [The NEED Project](https://www.need.org/) — K–12 lesson plans and activities
- [PBS LearningMedia – Energy](https://www.pbslearningmedia.org/subjects/science/physical-science/energy/)

## 💡 Teaching tips / Conseils / Tipps

- **[Energy-Charts](https://www.energy-charts.info/)** offers live, classroom-ready visualizations in German, English, and French — excellent for showing the real-time energy mix in class.
- **[Fraunhofer ISE](https://www.ise.fraunhofer.de/)** publishes freely reusable charts and figures.
- **[Our World in Data](https://ourworldindata.org/energy)** charts are downloadable and CC-licensed — ideal for slides.
